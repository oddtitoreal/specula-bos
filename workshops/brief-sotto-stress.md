# The Brief Under Stress

**Layer**: Modulated Brief Principle  
**Duration**: 90 minutes

---

## Objective

Surface the implicit value assumptions in the project brief before it becomes an operational mandate. Every brief carries within it, unnegotiated, the cognitive and disciplinary biases of whoever wrote it. This workshop makes them visible and transforms them from unconscious constraints into deliberate criteria.

---

## How It Works

The facilitator presents the existing brief to the Synthesis Circle. Each participant reads the document through their own disciplinary lens and notes undeclared assumptions. The group builds a tension map: what the brief takes for granted about users, technology, values, and system boundaries.

Each tension becomes a **How Might We** question that guides the rewrite.

---

## The HMW Protocol — Speculative Version

How Might We questions in design thinking open solution space. In Specula, they do something different: **they open the space of what is worth designing** before any solution is proposed.

The distinction is fundamental:

| HMW that optimizes | HMW that speculates |
|---|---|
| "How might we make onboarding faster?" | "How might we redesign onboarding for a future where users never sign up alone?" |
| Assumes the current problem frame is correct | Questions whether the problem frame is the right one |
| Seeks better solutions within the existing perimeter | Redraws the perimeter itself |
| Converges toward known categories | Opens territory no one is currently occupying |

A speculative HMW is not a better version of an optimizing HMW. It is a different cognitive act: it exposes the assumptions embedded in the brief as contingent choices, not necessary constraints.

### Step 1 — Assumption mapping (20 min)

Each participant reads the brief independently and notes every assumption they detect. Categories to scan:

- **Who is the user?** — Who does the brief assume? Who is invisible?
- **What is the problem?** — Is this the real problem, or a symptom of a deeper one?
- **What is the value at stake?** — Efficiency? Growth? Whose growth?
- **What future does it assume?** — Does the brief implicitly assume a stable, predictable context?
- **What does it refuse to question?** — What is treated as given that could be otherwise?

### Step 2 — Tension mapping (20 min)

The group clusters assumptions into tensions: pairs of opposing values or framings that the brief has resolved without deliberation. Each tension is named and placed on a shared map.

Examples of tensions a brief might hide:
- Speed vs. depth of understanding
- Scalability vs. relational coherence
- User autonomy vs. system guidance
- Short-term measurability vs. long-term identity

### Step 3 — Speculative HMW generation (30 min)

For each tension, the group generates two HMW questions:

1. **Optimizing HMW** — the question the brief implicitly already asks
2. **Speculative HMW** — the question that destabilizes the assumption and opens new territory

The facilitator's role is critical here: push the group to resist the pull toward familiar solution categories. A speculative HMW that can be answered immediately was not speculative enough.

> **Test for a good speculative HMW**: Does it make someone uncomfortable? Does it make the client slightly nervous? Does it open a space where no competitor currently operates? If yes, keep it.

### Step 4 — Brief rewrite (20 min)

The Synthesis Circle selects 2–3 speculative HMWs that most productively challenge the original brief. The facilitator rewrites the brief mandate incorporating these tensions explicitly — not as problems to solve, but as the generative field within which the project will operate.

---

## Expected Output

Modulated brief with:
- Explicit value assumptions (what the original brief took for granted)
- Named tensions (the unresolved choices now made visible)
- 2–3 speculative HMW questions that define the generative field
- Revised mandate incorporating the new framing

---

## Facilitation Notes

**On timing**: Do not rush Step 1. The quality of the HMWs in Step 3 depends entirely on the depth of assumption-mapping in Step 1.

**On resistance**: Clients sometimes resist speculative HMWs because they feel "too open." This resistance is itself diagnostic — it reveals which assumptions are most deeply embedded. Name it, do not resolve it prematurely.

**On documentation**: Every assumption surfaced, every tension named, and every HMW generated — including those not selected — goes into the Brand Memory as the first entry in the decision history of this project.
