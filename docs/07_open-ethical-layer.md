# The Open Ethical Layer

*Specula Future Crafting contributes to the discipline, not only to the client.*

---

For the BOS to have credibility, it cannot be a black box. One part of the architecture — the ethical part — is designed to be shared and made available as an open framework. Not out of rhetorical altruism: so that speculative branding becomes a shared discipline, and not a proprietary trick.

---

## Open vs. Proprietary

| Open | Proprietary |
|---|---|
| Ethical Gate (structure, guiding questions, exclusion criteria) | AI configurations and prompt libraries |
| Refusal Register (template and refusal typologies) | Scenario libraries and research protocols |
| Speculative Identity Canvas | Operational facilitation tools |
| Responsible AI Governance principles | Memory Architecture and JSON schemas |
| Method documentation (phases, principles, guidelines) | Client-specific work and case studies |

---

## The Behavioral Matrix as a Common Good

The Brand Governance Playbook that the BOS produces for each client follows a shareable structure: ethical DNA, behavioral matrix, decision flow, human escalation triggers, Refusal Register, integrity metrics. These six layers are not proprietary. The way they are populated with the specific values of each brand is.

### Allowed
- Contextual personalization with explicit consent
- Recommendations based on declared behaviors
- Automation of low-risk repetitive tasks
- A/B testing with privacy center disclosure

### Sensitive — requires review
- Inferences about emotional or psychological states
- Dynamic pricing based on behavioral data
- AI-generated content on sensitive topics
- Automation of high-risk complaint responses

### Prohibited
- Simulating a human voice without disclosure
- Using vulnerable data for commercial pressure
- Profiling minors without parental consent
- Dark patterns in unsubscribe flows
